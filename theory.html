Theory

Every ZSIGNAL composition is built on principles inherited from seven master artists and one immutable set of design constraints. This page catalogs the artists, the rules, and the engine that binds them.

Master Artists

Seven artists whose compositional principles are encoded into the ZIGNAL.ENGINE. Each archetype is a direct translation of their visual language into algorithmic rules.

Era I · FREE_FORM

László Moholy-Nagy

Hungarian-American, 1895–1946. Bauhaus master of light, transparency, and industrial precision. His “telephone paintings” (1922) and compositions like “A 19” (1927) pioneered art-by-instruction—the ancestor of generative art.

Era II · GRID

Piet Mondrian & Kandinsky

Mondrian (Dutch, 1872–1944) reduced painting to asymmetric grids of primary colors and black lines. Kandinsky (Russian, 1866–1944) composed floating geometric forms. Together they define the GRID archetype’s two strategies.

Era III · REPETITION

Paul Klee & Bridget Riley

Klee (Swiss-German, 1879–1940) treated visual composition as musical notation. Riley (British, b. 1931) created optical vibration through systematic size progressions. Their shared rhythm defines the REPETITION archetype.

Era IV · CONSTRUCTIVIST

El Lissitzky & Malevich

Lissitzky (Russian, 1890–1941) weaponized geometry in “Beat the Whites with the Red Wedge” (1920). Malevich (Ukrainian-Russian, 1879–1935) reduced art to pure geometric sensation. The CONSTRUCTIVIST archetype channels their dynamic tension.

Era V · COLOR_STUDY

Josef Albers

German-American, 1888–1976. His “Homage to the Square” series (1950–1976) is art history’s most rigorous investigation of color interaction. Nested, bottom-weighted squares. The COLOR_STUDY archetype faithfully encodes his proportional system.

Era VI · DOT_FIELD

Herbert Bayer & Victor Vasarely

Bayer (Austrian-American, 1900–1985) pioneered typographic experiments with mathematical grids. Vasarely (Hungarian-French, 1906–1997) created sphere illusions through dot modulation. Their legacy drives the DOT_FIELD archetype.

Era VII · ARABIAN_GEOMETRIC

Alhambra, Isfahan & Mamluk Masters

The unnamed artisans of Islamic geometric art (8th–16th century) created the world’s most sophisticated tessellation systems. Star-and-polygon patterns, girih tilings, and muqarnas vaulting—mathematical abstraction centuries before Mondrian.

The Rules

The Z-Signal genome: immutable constraints that define every ZSIGNAL composition.

Typography

IBM Plex Mono only. No serifs, no sans-serif. Monospace is the voice of the machine that generates the art.

Borders

2px solid, always. No gradients, no shadows (except the terminal shadow: 6px offset, 12% opacity).

Corners

Zero border-radius. Sharp corners only. Roundness is reserved for compositional shapes, never for UI elements.

Color Ground

Cream (#F2E8D5) background, charcoal (#1C1C1C) text. The warmth of aged paper meets the precision of typeset ink.

Gold Accent

Gold (#D4A84B) is the universal signal. It appears in every composition as a focal point—the message in the noise.

Determinism

Same archetype + palette + seed = same output, always. No randomness in execution. The art lies in the rules, not the rendering.

12 Primitives

Rect, circle, triangle, semicircle, quarter-circle, wedge, bar, trapezoid, dot, cross, arc segment, concentric ring. No curves beyond arcs.

6 Palettes

ZSIGNAL, Classic Bauhaus, Constructivist, Warm Earth, Cool Steel, Monochrome. Each derived from the master artist tradition it serves.

Frequently Asked Questions

Generative art is artwork created by a system—an algorithm, a set of rules, or a process—rather than by direct hand. The artist designs the system, and the system generates the composition. In ZSIGNAL's case, each artwork is defined by three parameters: an archetype (compositional grammar), a palette (color vocabulary), and a seed (specific instance). The system is deterministic: identical inputs always produce identical outputs.
Yes. Each artwork has a unique seed number that produces a one-of-a-kind composition. While the system could theoretically generate billions of variations, only 31 specific seeds have been selected by the artist as finished works. Each print includes the artwork's seed number, archetype, and palette—its complete genetic identity.
The newest archetype, ARABIAN_GEOMETRIC, draws on centuries of Islamic geometric art. These patterns use 6-fold, 8-fold, and 12-fold rotational symmetry—mathematical systems that predate European modernism by centuries.
