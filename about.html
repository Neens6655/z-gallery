Frequently Asked Questions

Generative art is artwork created by a system—an algorithm, a set of rules, or a process—rather than by direct hand. The artist designs the system, and the system generates the composition. In ZSIGNAL's case, each artwork is defined by three parameters: an archetype (compositional grammar), a palette (color vocabulary), and a seed (specific instance). The system is deterministic: identical inputs always produce identical outputs.
Yes. Each artwork has a unique seed number that produces a one-of-a-kind composition. While the system could theoretically generate billions of variations, only 26 specific seeds have been selected by the artist as finished works. Each print includes the artwork's seed number, archetype, and palette—its complete genetic identity.
Our fine art paper prints use 310gsm Hahnemühle cotton rag—the same archival-grade paper used by major museums and galleries worldwide. It's acid-free, lignin-free, and rated to last over 200 years without yellowing or degradation. Canvas prints use a premium poly-cotton blend, and acrylic prints feature 3mm crystal-clear acrylic with an aluminum backing.
All orders include free worldwide shipping. Standard delivery takes 5–7 business days. Orders are printed on demand at our production facility, carefully packaged in custom protective packaging, and shipped via tracked courier. You'll receive a tracking number as soon as your order ships.
Yes. We offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied with your print, contact us within 30 days of delivery for a full refund. We'll arrange a free return pickup. Custom-framed orders are also covered—we want you to love your artwork.
The Z-Signal genome is the set of immutable design constraints that define every ZSIGNAL composition: IBM Plex Mono typeface, 2px solid borders, zero border-radius (sharp corners only), the terminal shadow (6px offset), cream background, charcoal text, and gold as the universal accent color. These constraints aren't arbitrary—they derive from Bauhaus principles of geometric purity and functional typography.
